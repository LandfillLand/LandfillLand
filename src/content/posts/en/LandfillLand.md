When time began to move again, **LandfillLand** was not restored to its former orbit.

It remained at the instant where the interruption had occurred, like a wedge lodged deep in the foundation of the world. It no longer advanced, yet it could not be removed.

There was no turning of day and night here. No return of seasons. Light stayed at the angle where it had arrived. Shadows held unfinished shapes. Every change was preserved just before it could happen.

LandfillLand no longer generated life, but it was not empty.

It kept everything that had already occurred in an uncovered and unrevised state. Wind remained on the verge of scattering petals. Melody froze at the note closest to overflow. Leaves that had not yet fallen were lifted by time and held in the posture of almost.

Few visit LandfillLand often.

Yet it matters because it remembers what the living world cannot carry every day. It is the archive of divine memory, the vehicle of the past, and the quiet proof that even unfinished worlds deserve to be kept.
