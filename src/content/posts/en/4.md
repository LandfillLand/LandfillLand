When the erosion could no longer be ignored, the divine returned to WentUrc.

It would have been easy to erase the old world. A single silence could have folded its mountains, rivers, and unstable miracles back into nothingness. But the divine did not choose erasure.

WentUrc had suffered because it had once been loved without structure. Its disorder was not a crime. Its collapse was not a sin. It was the trace of a beginning that had been too bright to endure.

So the divine gave it a different ending.

The world was sealed. Motion slowed into suspension. Unfinished gestures remained unfinished, not as failure, but as testimony. The curse prevented WentUrc from becoming whole again, yet it also protected it from disappearing completely.

That is why the curse was mercy.

It allowed the old world to remain as memory: painful, radiant, and impossible to repeat.
