**Tianzelle** is not naive because she is sweet.

Her sweetness is chosen. It has passed through fear and still decided to remain warm. She knows that tenderness can be dismissed as weakness, so she turns it into a form of craft: careful, bright, and unexpectedly durable.

Where others see a broken place, Tianzelle looks for the smallest taste of possible joy. Not to deny grief, but to keep grief from becoming the only language left.

Her story is built from small restorations.

A cup shared at the right moment. A name remembered. A fragment of laughter carried across a room that had grown too quiet.

In Revaea, Tianzelle proves that sweetness can be a way of protecting the future.
