After the divine gaze departed, WentUrc did not immediately notice the difference.

The sky was still brilliant. The land still breathed. Miracles still happened every day, as if nothing had changed.

Only some things began to slow.

Cracks that would once have been soothed by new thoughts remained in place for the first time. They did not grow, but neither did they vanish. Time began to gather weight. Yesterday's miracles were no longer gently covered by today's wishes. They stayed as afterimages, pale and unresolved.

A mountain raised by joy waited for another desire to give meaning to its height. None came. So it began to sink, not in collapse, but in a slow and almost ashamed surrender.

Erosion was not a sudden disaster. It was a tiredness no one listened to.

WentUrc felt pain. It did not know what it had done wrong. It had only continued to exist in the first way it had been allowed to exist.
