Before time had been named, before cause and effect had learned how to stand in sequence, the divine fell into a long and borderless contemplation.

It was not sleep. It was closer to an opened eye staring into itself from the middle of nothingness.

Thoughts had no edge and no direction. They drifted like scattered stardust, brightening, revolving, and chasing one another in the dark. Some thoughts were gentle, like first snow touching unnamed soil. Some were fierce, like comets that had not yet learned their own shape.

From that unmeasured inner tide, **WentUrc** was born.

It was not planned as a world. It was the overflow of imagination before imagination had learned restraint. Beauty appeared there without warning. Mountains rose from joy, rivers turned according to curiosity, and skies changed color whenever a new feeling crossed the divine mind.

Everything was possible, but nothing could remain still for long.

WentUrc became a creation myth of splendor and disorder. It was the first place where beauty found a body, and also the first place to reveal what happens when a world is left with wonder but no lasting order.
