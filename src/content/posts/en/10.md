**Nianyu** stands near endings.

That does not make her cruel. In Revaea, death is not only a severing. It is also a threshold where fear can be softened and unfinished love can be held for a final moment without shame.

Nianyu's comfort is quiet. She does not decorate loss with false brightness. She sits beside it until it no longer has to stand alone.

Those who meet her may find that the last boundary is not a wall, but a hand extended in dim light. She listens to the words that could not be spoken sooner. She keeps them safe as the world grows distant.

Her story is a reminder that mercy is needed not only in life.

Sometimes mercy waits at the end, making departure bearable.
